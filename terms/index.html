9.2 Crime Data Limitations

Crime data displayed in ShieldNav is sourced from publicly available municipal open-data portals and may be subject to significant limitations, including but not limited to:

  • Reporting lag: Data may be days, weeks, or months behind actual crime occurrence dates;
  • Incomplete records: Many crimes go unreported; publicly available data reflects only reported incidents;
  • Aggregation and generalization: For privacy reasons, incident locations are typically reported at the block level or census tract level, not precise addresses;
  • Data errors: Municipal datasets may contain errors, duplicates, or misclassifications;
  • Geographic gaps: Not all cities or jurisdictions publish crime data, and coverage varies significantly by region;
  • Changing conditions: Neighborhood safety conditions can change rapidly and the app cannot reflect real-time conditions.

Crime data in ShieldNav should be used as one of many inputs to inform general caution — it is not a guarantee of safety in any area and is not a guarantee of danger in any area.
