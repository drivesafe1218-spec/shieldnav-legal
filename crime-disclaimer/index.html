1. What Crime Data Is

ShieldNav displays publicly available crime incident data sourced from official municipal and law enforcement databases. This data includes reported incidents such as theft, assault, burglary, vandalism, and other offenses as classified by local law enforcement agencies.

Crime data is used to:

  • Display incident markers and heatmaps on the map
  • Generate safety scores for routes and areas
  • Suggest potentially safer route alternatives during navigation

Crime data is one informational input among many — it is not a comprehensive picture of an area's safety, character, or the likelihood of any future event.

2. Data Sources and Limitations

ShieldNav obtains crime incident data from public municipal open data portals and law enforcement agencies. This data has significant inherent limitations:

  • Underreporting: Many crimes, particularly domestic violence, sexual assault, and hate crimes, are chronically underreported to law enforcement. The absence of reported incidents does not mean an area is free of crime.
  • Reporting delays: Data is published on a lag. Incidents from the past 24–72 hours (or longer) may not yet appear in our data feed.
  • Geocoding inaccuracies: Some incidents are geocoded to approximate locations or block-level centroids, not precise incident addresses. Map pins may be off by a city block or more.
  • Record quality: Incident reports may contain errors, duplicates, or charges that were later reduced, dismissed, or determined to be unfounded.
  • Classification inconsistency: Crime classification systems vary by jurisdiction. The same act may be classified differently in different cities.
  • Coverage gaps: Not all cities or jurisdictions publish crime data publicly. ShieldNav's coverage is limited to cities where data is available. See our coverage list in the app.
  • Not comprehensive: Open data portals do not include all incident types — federal crimes, certain juvenile records, and sealed records are excluded.
Important:

ShieldNav does not independently verify individual incident reports. We display data as received from official sources. ShieldNav is not responsible for errors, omissions, or inaccuracies in source data.

3. Not a Real-Time Safety System

ShieldNav is not a real-time crime alert system. The app:

  • Does not receive live feeds from 911 dispatch centers
  • Does not monitor police radio or CAD (computer-aided dispatch) systems
  • Does not provide alerts about crimes in progress
  • Does not have access to non-public law enforcement data

All crime data shown in ShieldNav is historical, typically ranging from hours to years old depending on the data source refresh cycle and the city. Route safety scores are based on historical incident patterns, not live conditions.

5. No Guarantee of Safety

ShieldNav's route recommendations and safety scores are informational tools only. No route is guaranteed to be free from crime, danger, or hazard.

A route with a high safety score may still involve risk. A route with a lower score may be perfectly safe at the time you travel. Crime patterns change. ShieldNav cannot predict future events.

You should:

  • Use your own judgment when evaluating routes and areas
  • Consider factors beyond crime data (time of day, weather, traffic, personal familiarity with the area)
  • Never place more confidence in the app's suggestions than your own situational awareness

6. Navigation Safety

Do not use your phone while driving.

Use audio guidance only while operating a vehicle. Interacting with the ShieldNav app while driving is dangerous and may be illegal in your jurisdiction. You are solely responsible for safe vehicle operation at all times.

ShieldNav provides route guidance for informational purposes only. Navigation directions may contain errors due to map data limitations, road condition changes, or routing algorithm imperfections. Always:

  • Follow posted road signs and traffic signals, which take precedence over app guidance
  • Obey all applicable traffic laws
  • Exercise independent judgment when navigation directions seem incorrect

7. Emergency Situations

🚨 If you are in danger or witness a crime in progress, call 911 immediately.

ShieldNav is not connected to emergency services. The app cannot contact police, fire, or medical services on your behalf. Do not use ShieldNav as a substitute for calling emergency services.

After reaching safety, you can report crime incidents through your local government's non-emergency line or online reporting portal. ShieldNav may eventually reflect newly reported incidents after they appear in public data sources.

8. Data Methodology

ShieldNav calculates area safety scores using a proprietary algorithm that considers:

  • Type of incident (weighted by severity)
  • Frequency of incidents within a geographic radius
  • Recency of incidents (more recent incidents weighted more heavily)
  • Normalization relative to the city's overall incident baseline

Scores are recalculated periodically as new data becomes available. Score methodology may change over time as we refine our models. ShieldNav does not publish the specific weights or formulas used in scoring calculations, as these are proprietary.

Data attribution: Crime data is sourced from municipal open data portals operated by the respective city governments. ShieldNav does not claim ownership of underlying incident data. City attribution is displayed within the app's crime coverage section.
